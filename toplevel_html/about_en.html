About the site

The Danish Society for Language and Literature, as a part of the Carlsberg Foundation financed Semper Ardens project Digitale Hovedstrømninger / Digital Currents (2016-19, read more here) has performed the work of providing a new edition of Georg Brandes’ Main Currents in Nineteenth Century Literature (1872-1890).

This work involves the production of a critical textual and annotated digital edition. The first editions of the six volumes of Brandes’ work serve as the textual basis, and the edition is supplied with a selective variant apparatus that illuminates the changes Brandes made in later editions. Added to this are detailed research-based introductions to the individual volumes of Main Currents, indexes of persons, works, literary characters and places, linking to the DSL’s dictionaries, search functions, a timelineof Brandes’ life and international activities, and various resource texts: German, English and French translations of the work as well as Brandes’ Explanation and Defense (1872) and autobiographical Memoirs 1-3 (1905-1908).

Also on the domain georgbrandes.dk is an exploration of Main Currents as a comparative literary and cultural critical event, which has been carried out within the parameters of the research project. The project’s experiments with digital methods of textual analysis are also documented.

The project leader for Digitale Hovedstrømninger / Digital Currents has been Lasse Horne Kjældgaard (Roskilde University). The vice project leader and chief editor of the edition and website has been Jens Bjerring-Hansen (University of Copenhagen).
