by Per Dahl

a. Book Description

Explanation and Defense was published in Copenhagen on April 18th, 1872. It was priced at 48 skillings.

Print run was 1250 copies.

[1] Explanation and Defense. | – | An Anti-Critique | by  | G. Brandes. | [publisher’s seal] | Copenhagen. | Published by Gyldendal Book Publishing (F. Hegel). | Græbe’s Book Printing. | 1872. [2] blank [3]-78 text [79] blank [80] blank.

Page measurements: 76 x 127 mm.

The text is set with antiqua, display settings with spacing. Aside from an introductory section (pp. 3-6), Explanation and Defense is divided into five sections, designated with Roman numerals. Citations and footnotes are set with a smaller type size. Pages 25-26 and 77 are modelled on Brandes’ childhood friend, the art historian Julius Lange (1838-1896); compare Georg Brandes: Julius Lange: Letters from his Youth, 1898, pp. 225-26. Explanation and Defense was Brandes’ response to the reviewers and critics who had in the daily press and journals addressed Emigrant Literature, which was published on February 8th, 1872.

b. Second Edition

Explanation and Defense was published in a second edition as a part of (pp. 387-433) volume 13 of the Collected Writings. The volume was published in installments between May 9th and May 15th, 1903. The first installment had a print run of 7500, but this eventually stabilized at 3500. Aside from orthographic revisions, the text itself is unchanged. German citations are translated, later observations are added to the text that parse and identify the pseudonyms of the critics.
